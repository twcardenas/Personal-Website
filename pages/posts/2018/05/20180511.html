Research

I to run some errands in Newton, afterwards I did some genealogy research while I was in the area. Sadly the genealogy room in Newton Library is closed on Monday so I was unable to go. However the Lincolnton Library was open. I made my first stop at this library.

My first thoughts was that it was one of the smaller rooms I've been to. As per usual, I spent my first ten minutes walking around getting a bit of an idea of what they had there. Then continued to look at the vertical files and family history books. These books are the ones unique to this library. My local library and genealogy library has majority of the land, marriage, and deed information.

Information I got from this trip was a lot on supplemental lines I am researching for their high crossover into my family.

I had to make a trip to Charlotte as well. My exam was not until 5pm so this day I decide to spend the time before on research as well. This time at the Concord Library. This library had a lot more than the Lincolnton library. One thing I found interesting was a book on my ancestors. It was highly documented and the way the author recorded the documents was quite interesting. Not many books had recorded data in the way he has that I've seen.

This week was a successful research. I made sure to back up my data today as I do every week. As for the following week I plan to continue going through the data I've gathered, validate and see what research on the Coast of North Carolina I need. I plan to head that way to visit family and will stop and research at Libraries in Onslow County and adjacent counties.
