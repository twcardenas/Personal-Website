Projects I'm working on


Genealogy & Neo4J

Genealogy and coding are some of my things to do. Genealogy has a tons of relationships. Ancestors, descendants, in-laws, etc, etc. Graph databases would be wonderful to store a visualize of this information as well as traverse. I have not worked with graph databases till now. I have read about them but am happy to finally work with some.

"Keep your data model rich! Don't be afraid to have as many relationships as you need. The power of graph databases comes from finding surprising results when you have strongly interconnected data."

The FindMyPast was by far my favorite. It was extremely helpful. The author provided a lot of example queries, actual data, and visuals that made understanding the topic very easy. I practiced with a variety of tools, Spring Data, Python using different drivers. My preference so far is with Python and the drivers. It has been for me to persist the data. The wide range of dependencies there are and different versions for Neo4J made learning a headache.

Neo4J provided many examples easy to learn and useful, even for drivers that they do not support. I am going to continue using Neo4J with Python for now. I may later go back and try again with Spring and Java.

Overall I really enjoy using Neo4J and will probably use it a lot more in my future projects.
